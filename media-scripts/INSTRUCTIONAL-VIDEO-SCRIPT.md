# MoodFood — Instructional Video Script
**Type:** Full how-to tutorial  
**Duration:** 4–5 minutes  
**Audience:** New users who have downloaded the app and want a complete walkthrough  
**Purpose:** Show every major feature in logical order. By the end, the viewer should be confident using every part of MoodFood independently.  
**Production tool:** NotebookLM (see `VIDEO-STYLE-GUIDE.md` for visual and audio settings)

---

## INTRO (0:00–0:18)
**Visual:** MoodFood splash screen fades in over a beautiful food photograph. Logo centred, then the tagline animates in.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Welcome to MoodFood — the app that matches your dinner to how you actually feel tonight. In this tutorial, I'll walk you through everything: building your profile, getting your first recommendation, cooking with Moody, and logging your meals. Let's get started."*

**Text overlay:** **MoodFood — Complete Tutorial**

---

## PART 1 — WELCOME & ONBOARDING (0:18–1:10)

### 1.1 Welcome screen (0:18–0:28)
**Visual:** Welcome screen with food hero photo. "Food that meets your mood."  
**Voiceover:**
> *"When you first open MoodFood, you'll see the welcome screen. Tap 'Build my food profile' to begin. This is the most important step — the more you tell MoodFood, the better it gets at finding you the right meal."*

### 1.2 The onboarding flow (0:28–0:55)
**Visual:** Rapid montage through 6 onboarding sections. Each section shows the food photo hero at the top and the white bottom sheet sliding up. Pill chips being tapped.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"The onboarding takes about 4 minutes and covers 6 sections: your dietary lifestyle and any allergies — these are hard safety filters that are never relaxed. Your palate — the flavours and textures you love and avoid, plus your spice tolerance. Your favourite ingredients and cuisines. Your relationship with food — the psychology behind why you eat and what comforts you. Your comfort foods and what drains you. And finally your kitchen setup — your skill level, equipment, and time budget."*

**Text overlay labels per section:** DIET & SAFETY | PALATE | INGREDIENTS | FOOD PSYCHOLOGY | COMFORT | KITCHEN

### 1.3 Completing onboarding (0:55–1:10)
**Visual:** Review screen. "Nice to meet you, Jessica." Summary card.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"The final step is a summary of your profile. Check it looks right, then tap 'Continue'. You'll be asked to create an account to save your profile, verify your email, and start your free trial."*

---

## PART 2 — ACCOUNT & SUBSCRIPTION (1:10–1:35)

### 2.1 Account setup (1:10–1:20)
**Visual:** Account setup screen. Avatar circle with camera badge. Name, email, password fields.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"On the account screen, add your name, email, and a password. You can also add a profile photo here — just tap the camera badge on your avatar. Your email will never be shared."*

### 2.2 Email verification (1:20–1:27)
**Visual:** Verify email screen. Blue mail icon. "Confirm your email."  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Check your inbox for a confirmation email. Open the link inside — in this pilot version, tap 'I've opened the link' to simulate that step."*

### 2.3 Subscription (1:27–1:35)
**Visual:** Subscription screen. Three plan cards. Blue 'Annual' selected.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Choose a plan — Annual at £120 per year, Quarterly at £36, or Monthly at £15. You get a 7-day free trial first. No card is charged during the pilot. Tap 'Start free trial' to enter the app."*

---

## PART 3 — THE HOME SCREEN & CHECK-IN (1:35–2:20)

### 3.1 Home screen layout (1:35–1:50)
**Visual:** Home screen. Annotated callouts: header, hero photo, check-in card, stat cards, quick links.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"This is your home screen. At the top, your name and a notification bell. Below it, the hero photo shows Moody's current top pick based on your last check-in. The card below it is where you check in each evening."*

### 3.2 Doing a check-in (1:50–2:10)
**Visual:** Mood pills being tapped — "Tired" selected, turns blue. Time pills — 30 selected. Energy slider dragged to 35%. "Find tonight's dinner" tapped.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Tap your mood — how are you feeling right now? Select how much time you have using the number pills. Drag the energy slider — lower means you want something easy and minimal. Then tap 'Find tonight's dinner'."*

### 3.3 Reading the results (2:10–2:20)
**Visual:** Results screen. Pick cards with food photos, time chips, safety chips.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Moody shows you the best safe matches. Every card shows the recipe name, cook time, how many people it's scaled for, and confirmation that it's safe for everyone eating tonight. Tap 'Not tonight' to skip a pick without it affecting your preferences."*

---

## PART 4 — RECIPE DETAIL & COOK MODE (2:20–3:05)

### 4.1 Recipe detail (2:20–2:35)
**Visual:** Recipe detail screen. Scrolling through ingredients, checking them off. Add to grocery. Log your version button.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Tap a recipe to open the detail view. You'll see the ingredients — tap each one to check it off. 'Add ingredients to grocery' pushes everything to your grocery list. You can also log a photo of your version of this dish right here — more on that in a moment."*

### 4.2 Starting cook mode (2:35–2:50)
**Visual:** Cook mode — dark green screen. Step 1 text large and bold. Recipe photo below. Previous, Start, Next controls.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Tap 'Start cook mode' for a distraction-free step-by-step guide. The screen stays awake. Each step shows you exactly what's active and how long to cook it. Tap the timer button to start a countdown inline."*

### 4.3 Finishing cook mode (2:50–3:05)
**Visual:** Finish overlay. Stars rating. Camera button "Add a photo of your cook". Log meal & finish.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"When you finish, rate the meal with stars. You can also photograph your cook here — Moody will estimate the calories for you, and the photo saves to your food log. Tap 'Log meal and finish' to add it to your diary."*

---

## PART 5 — FOOD PHOTO LOG (3:05–3:35)

### 5.1 Photographing a meal (3:05–3:20)
**Visual:** Home screen camera button tapped. File picker opens. Photo selected. "Moody is reading your plate…" spinner. Analysis card appears — dish name, 620 kcal, macro bars for protein, carbs, fat, fibre.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"The food photo log lets you photograph any meal — not just ones you cooked from the app. Tap the camera button on the home screen or in the diary. Choose a photo from your library. Moody analyses it and gives you an estimated calorie count and macros in about 2 seconds. Tap 'Save to diary' to log it."*

### 5.2 Viewing the food log (3:20–3:35)
**Visual:** Food log screen. Grouped by day. Each card shows the photo thumbnail, dish name, calories, macros.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Your food photo log is under Settings → Food photo log. It groups meals by day and shows today's running calorie total on your home screen. These are estimates — informational only, never medical advice."*

---

## PART 6 — DIARY, GROCERY & PLANNER (3:35–4:00)

### 6.1 Diary (3:35–3:43)
**Visual:** Diary screen. Weekly reflection card. Photo strip. Meal entries.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Your diary tracks every meal you've cooked. The top section shows your photo log strip — tap a thumbnail to review it. Below are your logged cooks with ratings."*

### 6.2 Grocery (3:43–3:50)
**Visual:** Grocery list. Items being ticked off.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"The grocery screen keeps a running shopping list. Add items from any recipe detail page. Tick them off as you shop."*

### 6.3 Planner (3:50–4:00)
**Visual:** Planner screen. Mon–Fri with recipe cards and empty slots.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"The planner gives you a week overview. Tap any day to add or change a planned dinner. It's designed to be loose — enough structure to help, room to change your mind."*

---

## PART 7 — SETTINGS & PROFILE (4:00–4:20)

**Visual:** Settings screen. Scrolling through sections.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"Settings is your control panel. Update your account and public profile, manage household diners, review your health trends, edit your psychological food profile, and manage your subscription. The Help section has a full FAQ and this tutorial in written form."*

---

## OUTRO (4:20–4:35)
**Visual:** Home screen. Moody AI panel opens. A recipe card slides up. Fade to logo.  
**Voiceover:**
> *"That's everything. One last tip — the floating sparkle button opens Moody, your AI dinner co-pilot. Ask it anything: pick the easiest option, help you with the step you're on, or find something in under 15 minutes. MoodFood. Eat for the way you feel."*

**Text overlay:** **MoodFood** · *Eat for the way you feel*  
**End card:** App store badge + website URL

## NOTES FOR PRODUCTION
- Each part should have a subtle title card: "Part 1 — Onboarding" etc. in Plus Jakarta Sans Bold, white on a semi-transparent dark overlay.
- Screen recordings should be at 430×932 (mobile portrait) with a soft drop shadow, displayed centred on the gradient background.
- Cut on natural speech pauses — never cut mid-sentence.
- Add subtle "tap" sound effects (soft click) whenever a button is tapped on screen.
- See `VIDEO-STYLE-GUIDE.md` for full NotebookLM spec.
