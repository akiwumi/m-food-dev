## 1. Product Thesis

MoodFood is an AI-driven, mood-first cooking assistant that **takes the drudgery out of dinner time**. The core problem is not a lack of recipes — it is *decision fatigue*. "What's for dinner?" is asked three times a day and, after a long day, the decision itself (not the chopping or the heat) is the hardest part. Existing apps are passive "tool sheds" — vast searchable libraries that force the exhausted user to do all the heavy lifting of searching, filtering, and planning, which leads to cognitive overload, repetitive meals, nutritional deficits, and unhealthy takeout. MoodFood inverts this: the user states *how they feel* (including energy/tiredness level), and the AI — branded **Moody** — instantly returns a small set of curated, chef-tested options matched to their emotional state, energy, skill, dietary constraints, pantry, and local grocery cost. It is positioned not as a recipe finder but as a **"personal food psychologist and sous chef"** — the empathetic *architect* that thinks for the user, rather than a *tool shed* that waits for input.

---

## 2. Target Users & Jobs-to-Be-Done

**Primary:** Adults ~25–45 who cook regularly but feel daily mealtime decision fatigue. Skews health-conscious professionals who want intelligent help, not just more recipes.

**Three repeated personas across the decks:**
- **The Busy Professional** — exhausted at the end of the day, defaults to takeout; needs instant, low-effort answers.
- **The Family Cook** — needs variety-aware planning, household scaling, and grocery coordination for multiple diners (incl. kids, allergies).
- **The Health-Conscious individual** — wants nutritional balance and variety "without the obsession"; struggling with repetitive diets.

Also called out: **"Paprika converters"** — home cooks who want organizational utility but are underserved on emotional/financial intelligence.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- *Emotional:* "Decide for me — I'm too tired to know what I want." Reduce mental load; feel cared for, not judged. Build a healthier relationship with food.
- *Practical:* Get a meal I can actually cook with my energy/skill/time right now; know what it costs; shop efficiently; cook hands-free; avoid food ruts; eat with more variety and balance.

## 3. Positioning & Differentiation

**Central metaphor — "The Architect vs. The Tool Shed."** Competitors (Paprika, Samsung Food, NYT Cooking, Mealime, SideChef) are passive libraries: *"Here are 10,000 recipes. Good luck."* MoodFood is the active consultant that assumes the user has no plan and tells them what to build: *"You are tired. Here is the answer."*

**Three pillars of differentiation:**
1. **Emotional Intelligence** — psychological mood profiling + the "tiredness setting." Competitors track *macros (math)*; MoodFood tracks *psychology and behavior*.
2. **Financial Context** — real-time local grocery pricing, total basket cost, and price-per-portion. *"A financial planning tool, not just a grocery list."* Competitors offer 0% real-time local pricing.
3. **Skill & Quality Awareness** — "we don't suggest soufflés to novices," and recipes are **curated/chef-tested** rather than AI-generated (avoiding the hallucinated, untested "culinary failures" of AI-generated competitors).

**One-line positioning:** *The only app combining Psychology, Finance, and Culinary Utility.* MoodFood matches market leaders on utility (cloud sync, web import, pantry, aisle-sorted lists) but exceeds them entirely on personalization and active AI assistance.

## 4. Core Value Propositions (ranked)

1. **Mood → Meal in seconds.** Tell it how you feel; get ~3 curated options. No scrolling, no fatigue. ("From 'I don't know' to 'That looks perfect.'")
2. **The Tiredness Setting (energy filter).** When exhausted, the AI filters out complex/labor-intensive recipes (no 2-hour roasts) and surfaces only what you have the energy to cook right now.
3. **Psychological Food Profile.** The AI learns what "comfort," "stressed," "celebration" mean *to you personally* — making suggestions feel "almost telepathic."
4. **Behavioral / nutritional guardianship.** Detects "food ruts" and nutritional deficits from the food diary, then gently nudges variety and balance — non-judgmentally.
5. **Real-world cost clarity.** Local pricing, total basket cost, price-per-portion scaled to household size.
6. **Flawless hands-free cook mode.** Screen-always-on, step highlighting, ingredient cross-off, auto-detected timers, multi-dish pinning.
7. **Utility parity.** Cross-device cloud sync, one-tap web recipe importing, pantry optimization, aisle-sorted smart grocery lists with quantity aggregation (1 egg + 2 eggs = 3).

## 5. The Emotional / Psychological Model

The defining intellectual property is the **Psychological Food Profile**, built in three stages:
1. **Assessment (onboarding):** Beyond "Vegan" or "Italian," the user answers psychological questions — *What is your relationship with food? How do you define "comfort"? Actual cooking skill? Time constraints?* Plus hard safety filters (allergies, dietary mode).
2. **Definition phase:** The user defines what specific moods mean to *them*. One person's "stress cooking" is baking complex bread; another's is making toast. "Stressed" might mean crunchy textures for one user and soup for another. The AI calibrates to these personal definitions.
3. **Real-time calibration:** Every session starts on the **Mood Screen** — input = *[tiredness level] + [emotional state]* (e.g., Tired, Energetic, Hungry, Content, Comfort-Seeking, Adventurous). The AI filters the database for complexity, time, and comfort profile → outputs a few curated options. Users can recalibrate mood definitions at any time from their profile (dynamic recalibration).

**Framing:** Food as emotional support and mental health, not a math problem. MoodFood is "a mental health tool for the kitchen" that "combines the utility of a smart organizer with the empathy of a psychologist." Branded "**Algorithmic Empathy**."

**Tone of voice:** Supportive, calming, non-judgmental. The signature line captures it: *"You're exhausted. Here is something simple, healthy, and cheap that won't make you want to cry."* Deficit/variety nudges are gentle ("You've had pasta 3 times this week. How about a plant-based protein tonight?"), never shaming.

## 6. Signature Experiences (the moments that define the product)

- **Mood check-in → instant curated suggestions.** The Mood Dial / tiredness slider, then ~3 high-match options (e.g., "98% Match Score") instead of an endless feed.
- **The exhausted-user flow.** Type "I'm exhausted" → Moody replies "Let's keep it simple. 15 mins, 1 pot. How does a Lemon Chickpea Orzo sound?"
- **Same mood, different energy.** Energetic → "Complex Roast Chicken (1hr 30m)"; Tired → "15-Minute Ginger Soy Stir-Fry."
- **Hands-free cook mode.** Always-on screen ("the screen — and the chef — never goes dark"), tap-to-highlight current step, cross off ingredients, auto-detected tap-to-start timers, pin multiple active recipes, metric/imperial conversion.
- **Gentle variety / deficit detection.** Food diary surfaces "food ruts" and nutrient gaps with a Variety Score and friendly intervention.
- **From inspiration to ingredients.** Real-time local basket cost and price-per-portion shown *before you leave the house*; aisle-sorted, auto-aggregated smart list.
- **The 4-step promise:** *Open app → Tell it you're tired → Receive the perfect simple, cheap, healthy solution → Cook with joy.*
- **User journey arc:** Onboarding → Daily Trigger (mood/schedule prompt) → Solution (curated recipes + list) → Execution (guided cook mode) → Reflection (rate the meal; feeds future personalization).

## 7. Brand & Experience Principles

- **Always feel calming and harmonious.** Repeated brand words: "happy, beautiful, colorful," "harmony in color choices," "promote good health choices." Tagline: *"Bringing harmony to the kitchen, one mood at a time."*
- **Mood-aware, warm palette.** The decks use organic greens + warm orange/earth tones; mood states get distinct colors (tired = cool blue/calm, energetic = yellow/sun, comfort = green/orange). The UI should feel emotionally responsive and warm, never clinical.
- **Moody, the assistant, is the primary control layer** — not a bolt-on chatbot. (Master plan: Moody "is the primary control layer of the entire experience.") The vision decks personify the AI as an empathetic sous-chef/"food psychologist" with a supportive conversational voice.
- **Flawless, frictionless interaction.** "Flawless" is a recurring requirement — the AI facilitates every option in the app so interaction feels effortless.
- **Imagery / mood:** diverse multi-generational people enjoying cooking and eating together; abundant colorful whole foods; bright, sunlit kitchens. Communicates connection, health, and joy.

## 8. Monetization Framing

- **Model:** Freemium → subscription, designed to convert trials into long-term loyalty. High switching cost / retention created by the accumulated, recalibrated personalization ("Mood personalization lock-in").
- **Free trial:** 7-day full-feature access — "experience the magic" and establish the daily mood habit.
- **Canonical pricing (subscription only — no lifetime):**
  - **Monthly: $10 / month**
  - **Annual: $100 / year** (≈2 months free; "Best Value" / most popular)
- **Value-justification narrative:** the local price-comparison tool *pays for the subscription* via grocery savings; the value of a "personal nutritionist + food psychologist" far exceeds the monthly price.

> **Lifetime removed (product decision):** the lifetime one-time plan has been dropped — MoodFood is **trial → monthly/annual subscription only**. This also fits the recurring-cost model (LLM + recipe/nutrition provider APIs make a one-time price economically risky).
>
> ⚠️ **Pricing inconsistency in the source decks (historical note):** the decks were NOT internally consistent — they showed lifetime at $180/$250/$280 and several used **€** (€12/mo, €120/yr). Canonical going forward is **7-day trial → $10/mo → $100/yr USD**; disregard the €/lifetime variants. (Funding ask also varied €80k/€100k; not product-relevant.)

## 9. Net-New Ideas from Vision Docs (NOT clearly in the build specs — don't lose these)

These appear in the pitch/vision PDFs and may extend beyond the seven-pillar master-plan inventory. Flagged for the team to consciously include or defer:

1. **Real-time LOCAL grocery pricing via store-inventory APIs.** Beyond a static grocery list: connect to local grocery store data for live pricing *and* local stock/availability ("In Stock"), driving total-basket cost and **price-per-portion scaled to household size**. Framed as a core differentiator ("0% of competitors offer real-time local pricing"). *Master plan has grocery automation, but live local pricing + inventory is a distinct, heavier integration.*
2. **Total Budget Calculation / spending hard-caps** (e.g., "Dinner under €15," weekly grocery spend cap). Explicitly a **Phase 2 / roadmap** item — recipe results constrained by a user budget. Positions MoodFood as a "household financial planning tool."
3. **Mood + Weather + Season context** for suggestions. One deck lists "real-time decision support: suggestions based on Mood **+ Weather + Season**" — environmental/seasonal signals beyond mood alone.
4. **Variety Score** — a quantified nutrient-variety metric (e.g., "92/100") with a weekly nutrient breakdown dashboard (protein/fiber/vitamins). A concrete, gamified surface for the deficit/variety engine.
5. **Sharing the *Psychological Food Profile* (not just recipes) as the core viral loop.** Friends share emotional food profiles to understand each other's relationship with food; the profile — not recipe links — is MoodFood's distinctive social object. Roadmapped to Q4. Plus exportable grocery carts/lists *to non-users* as organic brand exposure.
6. **Curated, human/chef-tested recipe library as a positioning pillar** ("AI matches the mood, humans verify the taste"). The explicit *anti-AI-generated-recipe* stance (vs. Samsung Food) is a brand/quality principle worth preserving in content sourcing strategy.
7. **Pantry optimization framed as waste reduction + cost saving** — AI prioritizes recipes using existing stock to cut waste/spend (a sustainability + savings angle on the pantry feature).
8. **"Mental Health in the Kitchen" launch/marketing positioning** — the deliberate framing of MoodFood as a *mental health tool*, not a recipe app, as the GTM narrative.
9. **Phase 3 "Deep Health" — medical/clinical diet integration** (doctor-recommended diets: low-sodium, diabetic-friendly; recovery/physical-recovery meals) woven into the mood engine. Long-horizon roadmap.
10. **Culturally-aware / global localization** captured at onboarding ("culturally aware products" to find region-appropriate recipes), with territory-by-territory rollout tied to grocery-API availability.
